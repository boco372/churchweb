23 Jesus looked around and said to his disciples, “How hard it is for the rich to enter the kingdom of God!” 24 The disciples were amazed at his words. But Jesus said again, “Children, how hard it is [b] to enter the kingdom of God! 25 It is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for someone who is rich to enter the kingdom of God.”,
26 The disciples were even more amazed, and said to each other, “Who then can be saved?” 27 Jesus looked at them and said, “With man this is impossible, but not with God; all things are possible with God.” 28 Then Peter spoke up, “We have left everything to follow you!”
29 “Truly I tell you,” Jesus replied, “no one who has left home or brothers or sisters or mother or father or children or fields for me and the gospel 30 will fail to receive a hundred times as much in this present age: homes, brothers, sisters, mothers, children and fields—along with persecutions—and in the age to come eternal life. 31 But many who are first will be last, and the last first.”

MUSIC IN OCTOBER at Holy Trinity Cathedral – Saturday 20 October at 7.30 pm Voices of the Organ: A Sonic Kaleidoscope. James Tibbles is one of New Zealand’s leading players of historic keyboards, with an active international performing and music career. James is Coordinator of Early Music Studies at the University of Auckland, where he teaches historic keyboards. Programme includes Widor, Alain, Brahms and more! Tickets on sale through iTicket.
The Bishop Selwyn Chapel – Saturday 27 October at 4.30 pm
Alison Dunlop and Janet Gibbs showcase the oboe in all its moods. Virtuosic and expressive in Handel, plangent and evocative in Schumann, edgy and modernistic in Hindemith, atmospheric and melodious in Harty. Come and hear an afternoon of gems in Auckland’s newest, and perhaps most beautiful, chamber music venue: the glass-walled, copper- roofed Bishop Selwyn Chapel. Tickets: Standard $40 Unwaged $25 can be booked at iTicket

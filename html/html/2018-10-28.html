BARNABAS FUND ART EXHIBITION 8 November 5 -7 pm. This art exhibition is to raise money to free a family from slavery.
The purpose it to raise money to free a family from slavery in Pakistan. Many Christians in Pakistan are trapped in “bonded labour” and it not only affects them, but their children and can even extend into future generations. The art exhibit will feature paintings for sale the size of the bricks made in the kilns in Pakistan as well as other vibrant artwork from several NZ artists. All painting are done by local artists. The paragraph below explains why we are doing this.
Brick-kiln workers in Pakistan, many of whom are impoverished Christians, are low paid. If someone falls sick or another kind of family crisis occurs, they have to take a loan from their employer, the Muslim owner of the brick-kiln. After this, money is deducted from their weekly wages to pay the interest on the loan, and this can go on for years, even for generations. As long as the debt remains, the family is “bonded” to their brick-kiln. They cannot leave to get another job and have to try to subsist on the reduced wages: it is almost like slavery. They may have no hope of regaining their freedom, and their children can’t go to school. We hope to raise enough money, about $4,000, to free two families from debt. It will make a huge difference to their lives and their children’s futures.

James 1:17-27;
 17 Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of the heavenly lights, who does not change like shifting shadows. 18 He chose to give us birth through the word of truth, that we might be a kind of first-fruits of all he created. Listening and Doing
19 My dear brothers and sisters, take note of this: Everyone should be quick to listen, slow to speak and slow to become angry, 20 because human anger does not produce the righteousness that God desires. 21 Therefore, get rid of all moral filth and the evil that is so prevalent and humbly accept the word planted in you, which can save you. 22 Do not merely listen to the word, and so deceive yourselves. Do what it says. 23 Anyone who listens to the word but does not do what it says is like someone who looks at his face in a mirror 24 and, after looking at himself, goes away and immediately forgets what he looks like. 25 But whoever looks intently into the perfect law that gives freedom, and continues in it—not forgetting what they have heard, but doing it—they will be blessed in what they do. 26 Those who consider themselves religious and yet do not keep a tight rein on their tongues deceive themselves, and their religion is worthless. 27 Religion that God our Father accepts as pure and faultless is this: to look after orphans and widows in their distress and to keep oneself from being polluted by the world.

Newsletter Information Note for the Parish from the Leadership Team
 I want to bring several matters from the Leadership Team to your attention for the Annual General Meeting and ask for your prayerful consideration.
1. The Leadership and Finance Teams are supporting an addition to our administrative staff in this year’s budget. We have budgeted for 5 hours a week paid Mandarin ministry support. It is an extension of the administrative support given to the congregation by Alison Stanton. While most of Alison’s work supports St Johns Parish as a whole, she does work to specifically support the English speaking fellowship. We believe by extending the administrative support specifically for our Mandarin Fellowship we are seeking to redress this imbalance. We will be presenting a motion to the AGM asking for congregational support for this.
2. We will be bringing an update of the Leadership Team’s working on the 5 year strategic plan.
3. We will be bringing a motion that we change what has been a choice made year by year of a Charity recipient for our Thanksgiving and Christmas Special donations to being fixed to our chosen charity for 5 years. We are asking for suggestions / nominations for that Charity from you, and that these be given to the Leadership Team for the November 6th 2018 meeting.
Blessings Lorraine
